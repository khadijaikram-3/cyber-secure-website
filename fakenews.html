Fake News Detection

Learn to spot misinformation and protect yourself online.

1
Question the Headline
Is it sensational, shocking, or too good to be true? Fake news often uses clickbait headlines to grab attention.
2
Check the Source
Is the website reputable? Look for official news outlets or organizations. Beware of unknown or suspicious URLs.
3
Verify the Evidence
Does the article provide facts, data, or quotes from experts? Fake news rarely cites credible sources.
4
Fact-Check with Others
Search for the story on fact-checking sites or other trusted news platforms before sharing.
Compare: Real vs Fake News Example
REAL NEWS
"World Health Organization releases new pandemic guidelines."
"NASA confirms successful Mars rover landing."
Reported by multiple trusted sources
FAKE NEWS
"NASA discovers aliens, keeps it secret!"
"Chocolate cures all diseases, experts say."
Sensational, unverified, or clickbait claims
Quick Tips to Detect Fake News
Look for spelling and grammar mistakes
Check the date of publication
Search for supporting evidence
Be skeptical of viral stories
Don’t share before verifying
Red Flags
No author or source listed
Emotional manipulation
Urgency to share or act fast
Old stories recycled as new
